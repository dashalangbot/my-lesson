Bridge to Grammar → You've just used already, yet and maybe just. Let's understand exactly when and how to use them.
② Grammar · 12 minutes

just · already · yet

Three signal words that always come with Present Perfect. Each one has a different feeling.

have/has + past participle

She has eaten.  |  They have arrived.  |  I haven't finished yet.

1
JUST just — very recently, a moment ago

"I've just got home." = I arrived a few minutes ago.

Position: between have and the verb  →  have just eaten

2
ALREADY already — sooner than expected

"She's already finished!" = Wow, that was fast!

Position: between have and the verb  →  have already seen

3
YET yet — expected but not happened; used in ❌ and ❓

"I haven't called her yet." / "Have you eaten yet?"

Position: always at the END of the sentence

Quick reference

WordUsePositionExample
just very recent action before verb I've just eaten.
already sooner than expected before verb She's already left!
yet expected, not done (❌/❓) end of sentence I haven't called yet.

Bridge to Truth or Lie → Last activity — the most fun one. Can you fool your classmates?
⑥ Truth or Lie · 13 minutes

Have you ever...?

One student picks a card and says "I have [done this]." using Present Perfect. Other students ask 3 questions in Past Simple to find out if it's true. Then vote: Truth or Lie?

How to play:

1. Student A taps a card → says "I have won a competition."

2. Others ask: "When was it?" / "Where did it happen?" / "How did you feel?"

3. Everyone votes: Truth or Lie? Then A reveals!
