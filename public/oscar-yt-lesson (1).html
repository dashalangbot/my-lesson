💡 The host uses both tenses in the video — PP Simple and PP Continuous. Let's look at exactly how and why.
Present Perfect Simple — result matters now
Present Perfect Simple
have / has + V3

Use when the result matters now — something happened and its effect is visible in the present. When it happened is not important.

"They have been the ones running the show since synchronized sound."
→ Они управляют этим с тех пор — и до сих пор. Результат виден сейчас.
"This has been the process for years, but is not without its critics."
→ Процесс существует давно — и сейчас тоже. PP Simple с for.
"We have yet to see if it works out."
→ Фиксированная фраза: результат ещё не наступил. = We still have not seen it.
"Every acting nominee was white — for the second year in a row."
→ Здесь Past Simple — конкретный год, конкретное событие. Заметь контраст с PP выше.
Present Perfect Continuous — process and duration
Present Perfect Continuous
have / has + been + V-ing

Use when duration matters — an action started in the past, continued, and its traces are visible now. Often with for / since.

"Critics have been saying for years that the process needs reform."
→ Говорят давно — и продолжают. Акцент на длительности.
"The Academy has been changing its membership rules since 2016."
→ Изменения идут с 2016 — процесс ещё не завершён.
"People have been noticing the lack of diversity for years."
→ Замечают уже давно — и сейчас тоже. Процесс продолжается.
⚖️ PP Simple vs PP Continuous — when to use which
Ситуация ✅ PP Simple 🔄 PP Continuous
АкцентResult / completed factProcess / duration
With numbersShe has won three Oscars.❌ Нельзя с числом
DurationHe has worked there for years. (факт)He has been working there for years. (процесс)
Completed?I have written the script. (готово)I have been writing the script. (ещё не готово)
From the videoThe process has been the same for years.Critics have been saying it needs reform.
⚠️ Common mistakes
❌ I have been seen this film three times.
✅ I have seen this film three times.
There is a number (three) → PP Simple only.
❌ She has been knowing him for years.
✅ She has known him for years.
"Know" is a stative verb — PPC cannot be used with it.
❌ They have changed the rules in 2016.
✅ They changed the rules in 2016.
Specific year → Past Simple, not PP.
❌ I have been writing the script — here it is!
✅ I have written the script — here it is!
The result is ready and you are showing it → PP Simple.
